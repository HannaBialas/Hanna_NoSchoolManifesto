No School Manifesto

1. No School envisions a rethinking of education for the twenty-first century.

2. No School aims to bridge the gap between culture and nature.

3. No School advocates a shift from what benefits the individual to what benefits the community and the entire planet.

4. No School defines creative learning as an individual process, akin to the artistic process, rooted in connecting with the inherent creative source in nature.

5. No School promotes a shift to creativity, recognizing it as the fundamental formative power in all of nature, which humans can tap into.

6. No School sees students as 'learners' and teachers as 'leading learners', both scholars contributing, at their respective levels, to this creative inquiry.

7. No School values the use of senses, intellect, and imagination equally for everyone, rejecting systems of punishment and institutions of control.

8. No School is a radical movement seeking to affirm the necessity of free education by challenging the economic foundations it rests upon.

9. No School opposes an economic system based solely on transactions between bureaucratic governments and conservative institutions.

10. No School advocates for an economic system where a network of teachers and students receives direct funding to create innovative forms of education.

11. No School represents a shift in education, focusing on individuals rather than on a dehumanized, economic-centered system.
